PropOps Copilot - Business Problem Statement
============================================

This project solves the operations bottleneck in property maintenance intake.

The core business problem is:
How can property management teams handle large volumes of messy, unstructured maintenance requests quickly, consistently, and safely without overloading property managers and dispatch staff?

Current pain points
-------------------

1. Too much manual triage
Requests come from portals, email, SMS/chat, and phone notes. Staff must read, interpret, categorize, prioritize, and route each request manually.

2. Slow response and dispatch
Delays in identifying emergencies or assigning the right vendor reduce tenant satisfaction, increase operational risk, and can raise repair costs.

3. Inconsistent decision-making
Different staff may classify the same issue differently, leading to uneven service, missed escalations, and policy non-compliance.

4. Knowledge trapped in people and scattered documents
SOPs, after-hours rules, vendor preferences, and property-specific exceptions are difficult to apply consistently at scale.

5. High-cost administrative work
Skilled staff spend too much time on repetitive coordination instead of focusing on exceptions, escalations, vendor management, and tenant experience.

Business value
--------------

- Faster first response
- More consistent triage
- Better emergency handling
- Lower administrative workload
- Improved tenant experience
- Better operational scalability

Value proposition
-----------------

PropOps Copilot helps property management teams turn unstructured maintenance requests into fast, consistent, policy-aware actions.

In simple terms, it reduces the time, cost, and inconsistency of maintenance request triage while improving tenant responsiveness and operational control.
